Beams and Frames

and how they're different

By Meghraj Goswami, 2022A2B41869H

Beam

  • Horizontal members for carrying vertical loads
  • Designed to resist shear force and bending moment
  • Typically supported at ends (simply-supported beam)
  • Steel reinforcement rods used since concrete weak under tension
  • Wooden laminated beams are also used

Frames

  • Holistic structure of beams and columns + support
  • Subjected to both vertical and axial loading
  • Columns are vertical load bearing members

Differences

  • Beams are horizontal members, whereas frames are constituted of both beams and vertical members
  • Beams are designed to resist lateral loads and bending moment, whereas Frames are designed to resist multiple loads and moments
  • For beams, a vertical load supporting member like a column or brick wall should be used to transfer normal load

Uses

  • Beams are used for plate girders, drain covers, roofing, balconies etc
  • Frames are used for rigid framework for multi-storeyed buildings, industrial structures, racks
